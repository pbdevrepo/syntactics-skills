# Article & Blog Writing Reference

Full frameworks for writing AI-optimized, SEO-friendly articles. Use this file when in
Mode 2 (Article Writing). Includes article types, structural templates, SEO elements,
and before/after examples.

---

## Before You Write: The Pre-Writing Checklist

Always establish these before producing any article:

- [ ] **Primary keyword** — What search term does this article target?
- [ ] **Search intent** — Is the reader trying to learn, compare, or decide?
- [ ] **Target audience** — Who is this written for? (e.g., small business owners in the Philippines)
- [ ] **Article type** — Which framework applies? (see below)
- [ ] **Target word count** — Based on article type and competition
- [ ] **Internal links** — Which 2–3 site pages should this link to?
- [ ] **CTA at the end** — What should the reader do after reading?

## Article Types & Frameworks

### Type 1: How-To / Guide

**Use for:** Teaching a process. High informational intent. Excellent for long-tail keywords.
**Word count:** 1,000–2,000 words
**Primary keyword pattern:** "how to [do X]", "guide to [X]", "steps to [X]"

**Structure:**
```
[SEO Title — How to [Do X]: A Step-by-Step Guide]

TL;DR: [2–3 sentence summary of the answer]

Introduction
- Hook: Start with the problem or question the reader has
- State what they'll learn by the end
- Why this matters (1 short paragraph)

What You Need Before You Start (optional)
- Prerequisites, tools, or context

Step 1: [Action verb + outcome]
[2–4 sentences explaining the step. Be specific.]

Step 2: [Action verb + outcome]
...

Step N: [Final step]

Common Mistakes to Avoid (optional H2)
[2–3 bullet points with brief explanations]

Conclusion
- Restate what was accomplished
- CTA: "Ready to [related service]? [Link to service page]."

FAQ Section (optional but recommended)
- "How long does [process] take?"
- "What if [common edge case]?"
```

**Example titles:**
- "How to Choose a Web Design Agency in the Philippines: 7 Things to Check"
- "How to Set Up Google Analytics 4 on Your WordPress Site"
- "How to Write a Project Brief for a Custom Web Application"

---

### Type 2: Listicle

**Use for:** Aggregating options, tips, tools, or examples. Very shareable. High click-through from search.
**Word count:** 1,000–1,800 words
**Primary keyword pattern:** "[X] best [things]", "[X] tips for [Y]", "[X] ways to [do Y]"

**Structure:**
```
[SEO Title — X [Things/Tips/Tools] for [Goal/Audience]]

TL;DR: [Summary of the list's main value]

Introduction
- Why this list matters
- Who it's for
- Quick overview of what's covered

## 1. [Item Name]
[2–4 sentences. What it is, why it's on the list, specific detail.]

## 2. [Item Name]
...

## [X]. [Final Item]

Conclusion
- Summarize the top pick or key pattern
- CTA to related service or next article

FAQ (if relevant)
```

**SEO note:** The number in the title should match the actual number of items. Don't clickbait.

**Example titles:**
- "7 Signs Your Business Needs a Website Redesign"
- "10 WordPress Plugins Every Business Website Should Have"
- "5 Differences Between a Web Designer and a Web Developer"

---

### Type 3: Pillar / Comprehensive Guide

**Use for:** Targeting a high-volume, competitive keyword. Becomes the hub of a topic cluster.
**Word count:** 2,000–4,000+ words
**Primary keyword pattern:** "[Topic]", "what is [Topic]", "[Topic]: complete guide"

**Structure:**
```
[SEO Title — [Topic]: The Complete Guide for [Audience]]

TL;DR: [3–4 sentence executive summary]

Table of Contents (linked)
1. What is [Topic]?
2. Why [Topic] matters for [audience]
3. [Core subtopic 1]
4. [Core subtopic 2]
5. [Core subtopic 3]
6. Common mistakes / misconceptions
7. How to get started / next steps
8. FAQ

## 1. What Is [Topic]?
[Definition paragraph — factual, citable, AI-extractable. This is what AI agents will quote.]

## 2. Why [Topic] Matters for [Audience]
[Business case — 2–3 specific reasons with data or examples]

## 3–5. [Core Subtopics]
[Each section: 200–400 words. Question-based H3s inside each H2.]

## Common Mistakes
[3–5 bullets with 2-sentence explanations each]

## How to Get Started
[3–5 actionable steps. Link to relevant service page here.]

## Frequently Asked Questions
[5–8 questions. Use FAQPage schema.]

Conclusion + CTA
```

**Internal linking strategy for pillar articles:**
- Link OUT to 4–6 cluster articles on related subtopics
- Link TO this pillar from all cluster articles
- Link to the most relevant service page at the conclusion

**Example titles:**
- "Web Design in the Philippines: The Complete Guide for 2025"
- "What Is Custom Software Development? A Business Owner's Guide"
- "Digital Marketing for Philippine SMEs: Everything You Need to Know"

### Type 4: Opinion / Thought Leadership

**Use for:** Building brand authority. Expressing a clear point of view. High E-E-A-T signal.
**Word count:** 800–1,500 words
**Primary keyword pattern:** Usually secondary — this type prioritizes brand credibility over volume

**Structure:**
```
[SEO Title — [Controversial/Interesting Claim]: Here's Why]

TL;DR: [State your position clearly in 2 sentences]

Introduction
- State the claim or position upfront (don't bury it)
- Brief context on why this is worth discussing

The Conventional Wisdom (and why it's incomplete)
[Acknowledge the common view — then challenge it with specifics]

Our Position / What We've Seen
[Draw from real experience: client work, project outcomes, industry observations]
[Use first-person plural: "In our 26 years building websites..."]

Evidence / Supporting Points
## Point 1: [Specific reason]
## Point 2: [Specific reason]
## Point 3: [Specific reason]

The Counterargument (and our response)
[Steelman the opposing view, then rebut with specifics]

Conclusion
[Restate position. Invite discussion. CTA if relevant.]
```

**E-E-A-T note:** Opinion pieces MUST have a named author with credentials. Anonymous opinion content
has very low authority signal. Add: "Written by [Name], [Title] at [Company] — [X] years experience in [field]."

**Example titles:**
- "Why Most Philippine Business Websites Fail at SEO (And It's Not the Content)"
- "We've Built 5,800 Websites. Here's What Clients Get Wrong About Web Design"
- "WordPress vs. Custom Development: The Answer Depends on One Question"

---

### Type 5: News / Update Article

**Use for:** Reacting to industry news, platform updates, algorithm changes. Shows freshness signal.
**Word count:** 400–800 words
**Primary keyword pattern:** Usually the news topic itself + date/year

**Structure:**
```
[SEO Title — [What Happened]: What It Means for [Audience]]

TL;DR: [What happened + why it matters to the reader, in 2 sentences]

What Happened
[Factual summary — 1–2 paragraphs. Link to the original source.]

Why It Matters for [Audience]
[Practical implications — 2–3 specific effects]

What You Should Do
[2–3 actionable recommendations]

[Company]'s Take (optional)
[1 paragraph opinion or observation — builds brand voice]

CTA
["Questions about how this affects your website? Contact our team."]
```

**Freshness note:** Include publish date in the title or H1 where relevant ("Google's March 2025 Update:
What Changed"). Update the article if the situation evolves — update the "Last updated" date too.

## Universal SEO Elements for Every Article

Always include and confirm these for every article before delivery:

### Title Tag (≠ Article H1)
- Include primary keyword, ideally near the front
- Under 60 characters (or it truncates in search results)
- Different from H1 is fine — title is for search, H1 is for readers
- Include a number, year, or qualifier when it naturally fits

```
✅ "Web Design Costs Philippines 2025: What to Budget"   [54 chars]
✅ "7 Signs You Need a Website Redesign"                 [37 chars]
❌ "Everything You Need to Know About Web Design in the Philippines and Beyond"  [too long]
```

### Meta Description
- 140–155 characters
- Contains primary keyword
- Answers "what will I find here?" — treat it as a mini-pitch
- Include a soft CTA if space allows

```
✅ "Wondering what web design costs in the Philippines? We break down pricing for brochure 
    sites, eCommerce stores, and custom apps. Get realistic numbers here." [155 chars]
```

### URL Slug
- Lowercase, hyphenated
- Primary keyword only — remove stop words
- Short and readable

```
✅ /blog/web-design-cost-philippines
❌ /blog/how-much-does-web-design-cost-in-the-philippines-in-2025
```

### H1
- One per article
- Contains or closely mirrors the primary keyword
- Can be longer/more descriptive than the title tag

### H2s and H3s
- H2s are major sections — each should address a distinct sub-question
- H3s are sub-points within H2 sections
- Phrase as questions where natural: "What Does Web Design Cost?" not "Pricing"
- Include secondary keywords naturally — don't force them

### Image Alt Text
- Descriptive, not keyword-stuffed
- "web design team reviewing wireframes at Syntactics office" not "web design philippines SEO"

### Internal Links (Required)
- Minimum 2–3 per article
- Link to: service pages, related blog articles, contact/quote page
- Anchor text must be descriptive (see anchor text rules in SKILL.md)

### External Links
- 1–2 authoritative external links per article (Google documentation, industry reports, etc.)
- Builds E-E-A-T and signals topical relevance
- Open in new tab

## Intro Formula (Hook → Problem → Promise)

The first 100 words of every article must do three things:

1. **Hook** — A question, statistic, or observation that immediately relates to the reader's situation
2. **Problem** — Name the specific pain point or knowledge gap
3. **Promise** — State exactly what the article will deliver

**Example (Web Design Costs article):**
```
Getting a website built in the Philippines can cost ₱15,000 — or ₱500,000. The range 
is that wide. [HOOK]

Most business owners don't know where their project falls until they've already gotten 
three wildly different quotes. [PROBLEM]

This guide breaks down exactly what drives web design costs in the Philippines, what 
you should expect to pay for different project types, and what questions to ask before 
signing anything. [PROMISE]
```

---

## Conclusion Formula (Summary → Insight → CTA)

1. **Summary** — Restate the key answer in 1–2 sentences (different wording from TL;DR)
2. **Insight** — One forward-looking observation or recommendation
3. **CTA** — Specific action with descriptive anchor text

**Example:**
```
Web design costs in the Philippines depend primarily on project complexity, platform 
choice, and the experience level of the agency you work with. A ₱50,000–₱150,000 
budget is realistic for most business websites.

The cheapest option isn't always the lowest risk — agencies with transparent processes 
and a track record of completed projects protect your investment better than bargain quotes.

Ready to get an accurate quote for your project? Request a free web design estimate 
from Syntactics and get a breakdown within 2 business days.
```

## E-E-A-T Article Checklist

Before delivering any article, check:

- [ ] Author name and role included (e.g., "By Maria Santos, Senior Content Strategist at Syntactics")
- [ ] Publish date present (and "Last updated" date if it's a revision)
- [ ] At least one external link to a credible source
- [ ] Specific, verifiable claims (not just "many businesses struggle with...")
- [ ] Company experience referenced where natural ("In our work with eCommerce clients...")
- [ ] No factual claims made without basis
- [ ] Article schema JSON-LD noted for dev handoff

## Before/After: Article Introduction

### ❌ Before (Weak Intro)
```
Web design is very important for businesses today. In this digital age, having a 
website is essential. This article will discuss web design costs in the Philippines.
```
**What's wrong:** No hook. Generic opener that could apply to any article ever written.
AI agents have no citable claim. Humans have no reason to keep reading.

### ✅ After (AI-Optimized + Human-Engaging)
```
TL;DR: Web design in the Philippines ranges from ₱15,000 for a basic brochure site 
to ₱500,000+ for a custom enterprise application. Most business websites fall in the 
₱50,000–₱200,000 range depending on complexity, platform, and agency experience.

---

Getting three quotes for a website and receiving numbers that are ₱100,000 apart is 
more common than you'd think.

The gap isn't random — it reflects real differences in what's being built, who's 
building it, and what's included after launch. Business owners who understand those 
differences make better decisions.

This guide breaks down the actual cost drivers for web design in the Philippines, 
typical price ranges by project type, and the questions to ask any agency before 
signing a proposal.
```
**Why it works:** TL;DR gives AI a citable summary. The hook creates tension. The promise
is specific. The reader knows exactly what they'll get.

## Before/After: Article Section Headings

### ❌ Before (Generic H2s)
```
## Web Design Pricing
## Factors to Consider
## Our Services
## Contact Us
```

### ✅ After (Question-Based, Keyword-Rich H2s)
```
## How Much Does Web Design Cost in the Philippines?
## What Factors Affect Web Design Pricing?
## What's Included in a Professional Web Design Package?
## How Do You Choose the Right Web Design Agency?
## Frequently Asked Questions About Web Design Costs
```
**Why it works:** Each heading is a question people actually search or ask AI agents.
Google's H2s get displayed in AI Overviews. They also signal topic depth.

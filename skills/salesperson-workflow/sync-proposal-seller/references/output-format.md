# Output Format - Proposal Cover

The Proposal Cover is prepended to the existing proposal file. It appears before the current cover page.
It is the first thing the client reads. Everything below it is the scope document.

---

<!-- BEGIN PROPOSAL COVER -->

## Why {Client Name} - {Project Name}

**Prepared for:** {Client Name}
**Prepared by:** {Sales Rep Name}, Syntactics Inc.
**Date:** {date}

---

### The Outcome

{2-3 sentences written from the client's perspective, not the vendor's.}
{What business result does the client get? What specifically changes for them?}
{Use their language. Avoid technical terms. Avoid agency language ("deliver", "implement", "leverage").}

**Example (do not copy - write this from the rep's Q1 answer):**
*Your procurement team stops chasing approvals over email and gets every purchase request reviewed and closed in under 24 hours. Finance gets a real-time view of committed spend without pulling reports. Managers approve on their phone without logging into a separate system.*

---

### Why Syntactics

- {Specific relevant experience or delivered project - name the type, not confidential client names}
- {Specific capability that matches this client's risk or complexity - one sentence, concrete}
- {What Syntactics does differently from a generalist vendor for this type of problem}

**Rules for this section (enforced in SKILL.md Step 1):**
- Each bullet must be specific to this project or this client's situation
- No generic credentials ("experienced team", "proven process", "agile methodology")
- If you cannot write something specific, the rep has not given you enough input - go back and ask

---

### What Happens Next

{One short paragraph.}
{What the client should do after reading this - specific action, not "reach out if you have questions".}
{What Syntactics does next if they say yes - contract, kickoff timeline, first deliverable.}

**Example (do not copy - write this from the rep's Q3 answer):**
*To move forward, book a 30-minute review call with us this week to walk through the scope and confirm any open questions. Once aligned, we send a contract and lock in the start date. Kickoff typically happens within 5 business days of signing.*

---

# Question Bank - sync-sales-discovery

Two phases. Qualification first. Scope second.

Use Phase 1 questions in the Call Agenda. Use Phase 2 questions in the Discovery Questions section of the brief.

---

## Phase 1 - Qualification Questions

Use these verbatim or adapt for tone. Never skip them.

### Budget

- Q-BU1: "To make sure we scope this appropriately - do you have a budget range in mind, or is that still being determined?"
- Q-BU2: "Have you done a project like this before? What did something similar cost you?"
- Q-BU3: "Is the budget already approved, or does that depend on what the scope looks like?"

### Authority and Decision-maker

- Q-AU1: "Who else on your side needs to be involved before you move forward on something like this?"
- Q-AU2: "Is this a decision you make, or does it go through a committee or procurement process?"
- Q-AU3: "Who would be the main point of contact on your team once the project kicks off?"

### Timeline

- Q-TL1: "Is there a hard deadline you're working toward, or is the timing flexible?"
- Q-TL2: "Is there an external event driving the timeline - a product launch, a contract renewal, a regulatory date?"
- Q-TL3: "If we started in the next 4 weeks, would that work on your end?"

### Competition

- Q-CO1: "Are you talking to other vendors as well, or is this an exploratory conversation?"
- Q-CO2: "Have you worked with an agency on a project like this before? What happened?"
- Q-CO3: "What would make you choose one vendor over another for this?"

### Problem Clarity

- Q-PC1: "Help me understand the core problem. What specifically is not working with how you do this today?"
- Q-PC2: "What happens if you don't build this in the next 6 months?"
- Q-PC3: "Do you have anything written down - a brief, a spec, an RFP - or are we starting from scratch?"

---

## Phase 2 - Scope Questions

Use these after qualification is complete. Adapt based on research findings.

### Modules and Scope

- Q-MS1: "Of the standard modules for this type of system - [list from research] - which are must-haves for Phase 1?"
- Q-MS2: "What features are nice-to-have but could be deferred to Phase 2?"
- Q-MS3: "Are there any capabilities you've seen in competing systems that you want to make sure are included?"
- Q-MS4: "What is the minimum viable version that would be useful on day one?"
- Q-MS5: "Is this replacing something, or is it a net new system?"

### User Roles

- Q-UR1: "Who are the different types of users who will use this system?"
- Q-UR2: "Does every user see and do the same things, or are there role-based differences in access?"
- Q-UR3: "Is there an admin who manages the system - user accounts, settings, data?"
- Q-UR4: "Will external parties (clients, vendors, contractors) ever log in, or is this internal only?"
- Q-UR5: "Are there approval or review actions that only certain roles can perform?"

### Integrations and Systems

- Q-IN1: "Of the common integration points for this type of system - [list from research] - which apply here?"
- Q-IN2: "Are there existing systems that must stay in place and connect to this one?"
- Q-IN3: "How does data move today - is it manual, via export/import, or does it sync in real time?"
- Q-IN4: "Is payment processing part of this, and if so, which gateway do you use?"
- Q-IN5: "Do you send automated notifications - email, SMS, in-app? Who manages those today?"

### Business Rules

- Q-BR1: "Are there approval steps or sign-off workflows baked into your current process?"
- Q-BR2: "What happens when something is rejected or goes wrong? Is there an escalation path?"
- Q-BR3: "Are there time limits or deadlines built into your process - e.g., approvals must happen within 48 hours?"
- Q-BR4: "Are there regulatory or compliance requirements that affect what the system must do or store?"
- Q-BR5: "What happens to data when a user leaves or a project closes - is there a deletion or archival policy?"

### Constraints

- Q-TC1: "Do you have a preference for technology stack, or are you open to our recommendation?"
- Q-TC2: "Where will this be hosted - your servers, cloud, or is that our decision to make?"
- Q-TC3: "Are there performance requirements - concurrent users, data volume, uptime SLAs?"
- Q-TC4: "What browsers and devices must this work on?"
- Q-TC5: "Is there an IT or security team on your side that needs to approve the architecture?"
